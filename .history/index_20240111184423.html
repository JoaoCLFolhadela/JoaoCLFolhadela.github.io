"Intelligent Self-Powered E-Tattoo for Human-Machine Interaction"

final grade: 19 out of 20 · FCUP · IMIFUP · Supervisor: André M. Pereira · Co-supervisor: Ana Pires

Developing my Masters thesis work has been one of the greatest joys of my academic course as it has been the most fun and fruitful work. I've obtained many skills and successfully developed an E-Tattoo prototype.

The E-Tattoo developed was able to localize positions where heat was applied. This is useful to detect either infections on our body (which locally increase temperature), thus being able to monitor wounds or other health-related issues that have deviations in temperature. More generally the device may also work as a powerless touch-sensor or to monitor any other surface due to the flexible nature of the E-Tattoos.

    This development concerned various steps, including:

  • Software development for automatic simulations in COMSOL with Python and tkinter;
  • Fabrication of the device through micro-fabrication techniques;
  • Developing software with kivy interface to extract data;
  • Data extraction automatization with Robotic Arm and Raspberry pi pico.
  • Data processing and training of Machine Learning models;
  • Deployment of the ML as a final system using kivy for simple GUI.
